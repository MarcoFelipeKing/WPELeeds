Our Story

The Department of Water, Public Health and Environment (WPE) was established in 2003 to address the growing challenges at the intersection of water management, environmental sustainability, and public health concerns. What began as a small research unit with just 6 academics has grown into a vibrant community of 25 academics and 50 PhD students.

Our department has pioneered innovative approaches to complex environmental challenges, earning recognition for our interdisciplinary research methodology that brings together experts from engineering, biology, public health, and social sciences. This collaborative approach has positioned us at the forefront of developing sustainable solutions for global environmental and health challenges.

Over the past two decades, we have expanded our research focus to include emerging concerns such as microplastics, resource recovery, and the health impacts of climate change, while maintaining our core expertise in water quality, sanitation systems, and indoor air quality.

Our Mission

To advance scientific knowledge and develop practical solutions for sustainable water management, improved public health outcomes, and environmental protection through rigorous interdisciplinary research, innovation, and collaboration.

Our Vision

A world where communities have equitable access to clean water, healthy environments, and sustainable sanitation systems, supported by evidence-based policies and innovative technologies that promote human and ecological wellbeing.
